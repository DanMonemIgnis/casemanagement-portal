What we are actually like to work with

There is a clinician in the room

When Miriam sits in your MDT she is not there to observe. She is a Band 7 RMN and our MCA Lead. She contributes clinical reasoning, leads on behavioural and therapeutic intervention and takes accountability for the decisions within her remit. That is not something most providers at our scale can offer.

We build on what is already working

For people who are settled and ready to move forward, we deliver personal care with therapeutic intent. We use the stability that already exists as a foundation for real progress rather than simply preserving the status quo.

We plan for the moments that break packages

Transitions are where most care falls apart. Child to adult services. Hospital to community. We plan for continuity from the very first conversation so the person does not lose what they have built when the handover comes.

You will not need to manage us

We take full ownership of our role. Reporting lines are clear, escalation contacts are agreed before we start and updates come to you without prompting. We fit around your existing structure rather than adding to your workload.

What is Ignis's safeguarding escalation pathway?▼
Any safeguarding concern is escalated immediately to our Designated Safeguarding Lead, Daniel Monem. We follow local authority thresholds and make referrals to the relevant MASH or statutory team within the required timeframes. All incidents are logged in real time on our PASS care management system, reviewed and reported in line with our QCS safeguarding policy. Patterns and trends are RAG rated and feed into our monthly clinical governance review.
Who is the Designated Safeguarding Lead?▼
Daniel Monem, our Nominated Individual and Director, holds the DSL role. Daniel has a Level 5 Designated Safeguarding Lead qualification and Safeguarding Children and Adults Level 2 and 3 certificates through Reed. He is directly contactable by any professional involved in a case on 07494 696 305. Miriam Chengeto Cummings, our Registered Manager and Band 7 RMN, is also DSL qualified and provides clinical safeguarding oversight day to day.
How does Ignis handle a concern involving a third party?▼
We follow local multi-agency safeguarding procedures, notify the relevant statutory body and maintain detailed records of every action taken and decision made. We do not investigate concerns ourselves. We refer, record and cooperate fully with any statutory investigation. Everything is documented within PASS with a complete audit trail.
What if someone has a concern about Ignis itself?▼
Any concern about Ignis, whether raised by a professional, a family member or the person we support, can be directed to Daniel Monem on 07494 696 305. All concerns are acknowledged within 24 to 48 hours, logged, investigated independently of the subject and responded to with clear outcomes. Where it is appropriate, we will actively support escalation to the Local Authority, CQC or the Local Government and Social Care Ombudsman.

How does Ignis approach risk?▼
We work within a positive risk management framework because we believe that managed risk is what supports autonomy, dignity and real therapeutic progress. All risk assessments are person centred, reviewed regularly and shared with the MDT. Restriction is never our default response. We plan, record and review, and every risk decision is RAG rated within our governance system. Red rated decisions trigger an immediate clinical review led by Miriam.
What is a PBS plan and does Ignis use them?▼
Yes. Where behaviours of concern are present, we develop a Positive Behaviour Support plan in collaboration with the person, their family and the wider MDT. These plans are led by Miriam in her clinical role, are evidence based and comply with NICE guidance NG11. Behaviour is always understood functionally before any form of restriction is considered, and plans are reviewed at every clinical governance cycle.
How are restrictive practices governed?▼
Any restrictive practice requires documented justification, MDT agreement and, where relevant, authorisation under the Mental Capacity Act or Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards. All restrictions are logged in PASS, linked directly to the relevant MCA decision and reviewed at every care review. We actively work to reduce restrictive practices over time, and restraint usage is reviewed at director level every month.

Who holds the MCA lead at Ignis?▼
Miriam Chengeto Cummings, our Registered Manager and Band 7 RMN, is our designated MCA Lead. She holds full accountability for the quality, defensibility and oversight of all capacity and best interests decisions across every package we hold.
How does Ignis make and record best interests decisions?▼
Capacity is assessed on a decision specific basis in line with the Mental Capacity Act 2005. Where a person lacks capacity for a particular decision, a formal best interests process is triggered. This involves MDT input, the person's family or advocates where appropriate and careful consideration of the person's past and present wishes, beliefs and values. All best interests meeting minutes are documented within PASS and every decision is recorded on our internal MCA Tracker with the rationale, MDT involvement and review dates clearly noted.
How does Ignis handle Deprivation of Liberty?▼
Where a DoLS authorisation is in place, we work within its conditions and record our compliance. Where we identify a potential deprivation that has not yet been authorised, we notify the relevant supervisory body and the person's legal representative straight away. All DoLS related decisions are logged and linked to our MCA governance framework.
How are MCA decisions monitored over time?▼
All decisions are RAG rated within our governance system. Red rated decisions trigger an immediate review. Amber decisions are subject to scheduled MDT oversight. Green decisions follow routine review cycles. Miriam leads a weekly governance review that cross references MCA decisions, incidents, restraint usage and safeguarding triggers together, so that nothing drifts without scrutiny.

What happens if the assigned worker is off sick?▼
We do not send unfamiliar faces. Every person we support has a core team and a secondary bank team who have already been introduced, have shadowed the package and have read the care plan, risk profile, PBS strategy and daily routines before they ever work independently. If someone is off sick, cover comes from that known secondary team first. In the rare event that further cover is needed, we draw from our own pre-vetted complex care network rather than open market agency supply.
Are staff directly employed or agency?▼
The primary workforce is Ignis controlled. Agency use is governed by a formal policy and requires authorisation from our Registered Manager. We draw only from an approved agency list and use agency support in exceptional circumstances only. Regardless of how a member of staff comes to us, they go through Ignis vetting, compliance checks and clinical briefing before working with any of the people we support.
What are the staffing ratios?▼
Ratios are set based on clinical need rather than a generic formula. Most packages begin at 1:1. Where presentations are more complex, involve higher risk or significant behavioural need, ratios of 2:1 or 3:1 are applied. Every ratio is defined within the care and risk management plan, justified through functional behaviour assessment and MDT input and kept under review through our RAG governance and incident data.
How is recruitment governed?▼
All recruitment follows a structured, values based process fully aligned to CQC Regulation 19, the Fit and Proper Persons requirement. Every appointment includes a full DBS check, a minimum of two references, right to work verification, qualifications check and a structured interview conducted by at least two people. No member of staff begins working until every compliance check is complete.

How does Ignis work with financial deputies?▼
We provide complete transparency across all costs, services delivered and any proposed changes to a care package. Financial deputies receive itemised reporting at whatever frequency has been agreed and are included in any care review that could affect funding. We hold no funds on behalf of the people we support. There are no hidden uplifts and no retrospective charges.
Is Ignis prepared for Court of Protection cases?▼
Yes. We maintain detailed, dated and auditable records of all care decisions, capacity assessments, best interests decisions and MDT communications. Everything is held within PASS and can be made available to legal representatives through the appropriate channels. Our MCA Tracker, RAG governance system and incident logs together create a complete and defensible record of every significant decision made in relation to any person we support.
What funding routes does Ignis work with?▼
We currently work primarily with case manager led packages including Court of Protection and private case management arrangements. We are expanding to include local authority commissioned packages, NHS Continuing Healthcare, direct payments and private self funders. All fee structures are transparent and, where required, built to align with framework rates.

What is the complaints process?▼
Any complaint, whether made verbally or in writing, is directed to Daniel Monem, our Nominated Individual, on 07494 696 305. We acknowledge all complaints within two working days and provide a full written response within 20 working days. Every complaint is logged, investigated by someone independent of the matter, reviewed at director level and responded to with clear outcomes. Where a complaint also involves a safeguarding concern, both processes run in parallel. Neither holds the other back.
How does Ignis respond if legal action is raised?▼
We keep comprehensive, contemporaneous records of everything we deliver, every decision we make and every incident we report. If legal action is raised, all records are preserved and made available through the proper legal channels. We carry full public liability and professional indemnity insurance. Our PASS system, MCA Tracker, incident logs and RAG governance structure are built to produce a defensible and transparent audit trail at any point.
What happens if Ignis needs to end a package?▼
Ending a package is always a last resort. Before any notice is served, we are required to exhaust every alternative, including care plan review, MDT input, PBS strategies and, where relevant, fee discussions. All stakeholders are involved throughout. A minimum of 28 days written notice is given, except in the rare situations where an immediate safety risk requires a faster response. Care continues in full throughout the notice period and we actively support the transition to a new provider so that there is no gap in provision.
