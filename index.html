What is Ignis's safeguarding escalation pathway?▼
Any safeguarding concern is escalated immediately to our Designated Safeguarding Lead, Daniel Monem. We follow local authority thresholds and make referrals to the relevant MASH or statutory team within required timeframes. All incidents are logged in real time on PASS, reviewed, and reported in line with our QCS safeguarding policy. Patterns and trends are RAG-rated and fed into monthly clinical governance review.
Who is the Designated Safeguarding Lead?▼
Daniel Monem, Nominated Individual and Director. Daniel holds a Level 5 Designated Safeguarding Lead qualification and Safeguarding Children and Adults Level 2 and 3 certificates (Reed). Directly contactable on 07494 696 305. Miriam Chengeto Cummings (Registered Manager, Band 7 RMN) is also DSL qualified and provides clinical safeguarding oversight day-to-day.
How does Ignis handle a concern involving a third party?▼
We follow local multi-agency safeguarding procedures, notify the relevant statutory body, and maintain detailed records of all actions taken and decisions made. We do not investigate concerns ourselves — we refer, record, and cooperate fully with any statutory investigation. All actions are documented within PASS with a full audit trail.
What if there is a concern about Ignis itself?▼
Any concern about Ignis — from a professional, family member, or the person we support — can be raised directly with Daniel Monem on 07494 696 305. All concerns are acknowledged within 24–48 hours, logged, investigated independently of the subject, and responded to with clear outcomes. Where appropriate, we actively support escalation to the Local Authority, CQC, or the Local Government and Social Care Ombudsman.

How does Ignis approach risk?▼
We use a positive risk management framework — recognising that managed risk supports autonomy, dignity, and therapeutic progress. All risk assessments are person-centred, reviewed regularly, and shared with the MDT. We do not restrict as a default; we plan, record, and review. All risk decisions are RAG-rated, with red-rated decisions triggering immediate clinical review led by Miriam.
What is a PBS plan and does Ignis use them?▼
Yes. Where behaviours of concern are present, Ignis develops a Positive Behaviour Support plan in collaboration with the person, their family, and the wider MDT. Led by Miriam as our clinical lead, PBS plans are evidence-based, comply with NICE guidance NG11, and are reviewed at each clinical governance cycle. Behaviour is always understood functionally before any restriction is considered.
How are restrictive practices governed?▼
Any restrictive practice requires documented justification, MDT agreement, and — where relevant — authorisation under the Mental Capacity Act or Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards. All restrictions are logged in PASS, linked directly to the relevant MCA decision, reviewed at every care review, and actively reduced over time. Restraint usage is reviewed at director level monthly.

Who holds the MCA lead at Ignis?▼
Mrs Miriam Chengeto Cummings, Registered Manager and Band 7 RMN, is the designated MCA Lead. She holds accountability for the quality, defensibility, and oversight of all capacity and best interests decisions across every package.
How does Ignis make and record best interests decisions?▼
Capacity is assessed decision-specifically in line with the Mental Capacity Act 2005. Where capacity is lacking, a formal Best Interests process is triggered — involving MDT input, family or advocates where appropriate, and full consideration of the person's past and present wishes, beliefs, and values. All Best Interests meeting minutes are documented in PASS and every decision is logged on our internal MCA Tracker with rationale, MDT involvement, and review dates.
How does Ignis handle Deprivation of Liberty?▼
Where a DoLS authorisation is in place, Ignis works within its conditions and records compliance. Where we identify a potential deprivation not yet authorised, we notify the relevant supervisory body and the person's legal representative immediately. All DoLS decisions are logged and linked to the MCA governance framework.
How are MCA decisions monitored over time?▼
All decisions are RAG-rated. Red-rated decisions trigger immediate review. Amber decisions are subject to scheduled MDT oversight. Green decisions follow routine review cycles. Weekly governance review led by Miriam cross-references MCA decisions, incidents, restraint usage, and safeguarding triggers to ensure nothing drifts without scrutiny.

What happens if the assigned worker is off sick?▼
Ignis does not operate a stranger model. Every service user has a core team and a pre-introduced secondary bank team — staff who have shadowed the package, read the care plan, risk profile, PBS strategy, and daily routines before working independently. In the event of sickness, cover is drawn from this known secondary team first. Emergency cover comes from Ignis's pre-vetted complex care database — not open-market agency.
Are staff directly employed or agency?▼
Primary delivery is through Ignis's own controlled workforce. Agency use is governed by a formal policy (PC21) — it requires RM authorisation, draws only from an approved agency list, and is used in exceptional circumstances only. All staff, regardless of source, go through Ignis vetting, compliance checks, clinical briefing, and are onboarded to our governance framework and PASS system before working with any service user.
What are the staffing ratios?▼
Ratios are needs-led and clinically justified, not generic. Typically 1:1 for baseline support, 2:1 or 3:1 for high-risk, behavioural, or complex clinical presentations. Ratios are defined in the care and risk management plan, justified through functional behaviour assessment and MDT input, and reviewed via RAG governance and incident data.
How is recruitment governed?▼
All recruitment follows a structured, values-based process aligned to CQC Regulation 19 (Fit and Proper Persons). This includes full DBS check, minimum two references, right to work verification, qualifications check, and structured interview by a minimum of two assessors. No staff member starts before all compliance checks are complete.

How does Ignis work with financial deputies?▼
We provide full transparency on all costs, services delivered, and any proposed changes to a care package. Financial deputies receive itemised reporting at the agreed frequency and are included in any care review that may affect funding. We hold no funds on behalf of service users. Our fee structure carries no hidden uplifts or retrospective charges.
Is Ignis prepared for Court of Protection cases?▼
Yes. We maintain detailed, dated, and auditable records of all care decisions, capacity assessments, best interests decisions, and MDT communications — all held within PASS and available to legal representatives through appropriate channels. Our MCA Tracker, RAG governance system, and incident logs create a complete, defensible record of every significant decision made in relation to a service user.
What funding routes does Ignis work with?▼
Ignis currently works primarily with case manager-led packages including Court of Protection and private case management. We are expanding to local authority commissioned packages, NHS Continuing Healthcare, direct payments, and private self-funders. All fee structures are transparent and built to align with framework rates where required, with clinical justification provided for enhanced packages.

What is the complaints process?▼
Any complaint — verbal or written — is directed to Daniel Monem, Nominated Individual, on 07494 696 305. All complaints are acknowledged within 2 working days and receive a full written response within 20 working days. Every complaint is logged, investigated independently of the subject, reviewed at director level, and responded to with clear outcomes. Where a complaint involves a safeguarding concern, both processes run in parallel.
How does Ignis respond if legal action is raised?▼
We maintain comprehensive, contemporaneous records of all care delivered, decisions made, and incidents reported. In the event of a legal claim, all records are preserved and made available through proper legal channels. Ignis holds full public liability and professional indemnity insurance. Our PASS system, MCA Tracker, incident logs, and RAG governance system are specifically designed to produce a defensible audit trail.
What happens if Ignis needs to end a package?▼
Termination is a last resort. Before any notice is served, Ignis must first exhaust all alternatives — care plan review, MDT input, PBS strategies, and where relevant, fee discussions. All stakeholders are engaged: the service user, family, advocates, commissioners, and housing providers. A minimum of 28 days' written notice is given. Care continues throughout the notice period and Ignis actively supports transition to a new provider with no gap in provision.
